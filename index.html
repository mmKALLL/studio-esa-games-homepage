The goal: Meaningful strategic decisions

I'm running an experimental studio aiming to create games with unprecedented depth.

For a decision to be truly meaningful, it must be more than just profound, asymmetric, or balanced. It must evoke curiosity in the player, invite them to ponder scenarios with unique outcomes, and require true insight to achieve optimal play. The results should surprise without being unfair, providing delightful experiences even after hundreds of hours of gameplay.

I'm tired of games that are solvable with simple heuristics, and want to demonstrate what strategic thinking really is all about.
